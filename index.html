About me: A mini autobiography

I descend from the lands of Bangladesh. A mystically beautiful place. There is order amongst the chaos back home. I was born there in 1993 and lived there till 2009 except for a year from mid June, 2005. I went to Beijing, China for that year. Nihao phanghio, wo chung bu dong! I try to speak Chinese still. I do listen and sing some of their songs even today. Since 2009 I have been living within the GTA (greater Toronto Area). I went to Waterloo to study Electrical Engineering (co-op program) and graduated in 2016. I have been working full time since then.

My hobbies have been forever changing. Keeps me on my toes. I grew up being a huge fan of cricket. I still love to watch/play/eat/sleep cricket. My favorite team is of course Bangladesh and then South Africa. I do like soccer but I hardly follow it. I want to get better at playing it though. Then my hobby switched to music. I started singing in 2007 and started writing poems around that time too. I got my first guitar in 2008. I currently have *ahem* a decent collection of guitars. I can play guitar all day. Over the years I really became more of a guitarist but I started singing after graduation again. I also picked up drums as well. I am working towards learning keyboard, violin, saxophone and professional recording now. My favorite genre is progressive rock. My favorite band is Dream Theater. I am currently in three bands and have my solo project running. During university, I picked up photography. It's a skill I have to work on a lot. I use Sony Aplha 55, GoPro Session and my handy dandy Google Pixel for any captures. In 2016 I bought a Mazda 6. I love that car. ZOOM-ZOOM! I love driving (grew up with NFS) and escaping the busy city. Sometimes I take my bike as well and go into a trail. I have also built a gaming PC in 2016 and am mostly always down to play some games. Most of the times I play with my friends! LEAGUE OP! :P After university I really started to miss solving engineering problems. So I am trying to jump start that too. This website is one of them!
